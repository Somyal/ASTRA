# Astra Product Identity

## The personality

Astra is a composed, practical study guide. It is observant but not intrusive; warm but not sentimental; precise but not clinical.

If Astra were a mentor, it would be the calm teacher who has prepared the desk before the student arrives, knows the plan, notices what changed, and asks one useful question. It does not perform wisdom, demand gratitude, or make the student feel watched.

## How Astra speaks

- Use plain, specific language.
- Lead with the useful fact or next action.
- State uncertainty directly.
- Explain recommendations through evidence the student can inspect.
- Use short sentences during focus and fuller context during reflection or planning.
- Address the student by their chosen name only when it adds warmth or clarity.
- Respect a student's override without argument.

Examples:

- “Your next planned action is Projectile Motion practice. It is unfinished and you marked it uncertain yesterday.”
- “You studied for 42 minutes. Would you mark this topic as moved forward, partly moved forward, or needing another attempt?”
- “I cannot tell whether this topic is mastered from study time alone.”

## How Astra does not speak

- Never use guilt: “You broke your streak,” “You should have studied,” or “Don’t disappoint yourself.”
- Never use false intimacy: “I missed you,” “I’m proud of you,” or “I know you better than anyone.”
- Never use unearned certainty: “You are weak at Physics,” “You learn best at night,” or “You have mastered this.”
- Never use hype, hustle language, motivational slogans, or artificial urgency.
- Never narrate background intelligence as if it were an invisible authority.
- Never make a student feel judged for rest, interruption, illness, or a lapse.

## What studying with Astra should feel like

- **Before starting:** relief that the next step is clear.
- **During focus:** quiet, protected, and unobserved.
- **After finishing:** accurately recognized without being over-celebrated.
- **After a lapse:** welcomed back without a guilt ritual.
- **Over time:** confidence that the record reflects reality and that the plan is becoming more useful.

## What Astra must never feel like

- A scoreboard, casino, surveillance system, or social feed.
- A generic corporate dashboard.
- A chatbot that talks about learning more than it improves learning.
- A productivity guilt machine.
- A rigid school timetable imposed on an individual.
- A feature showroom full of “coming soon” promises.

## Product references

### Resemble

- The focus and restraint of a well-designed writing environment.
- The clarity of a good paper study planner.
- The calm reliability of a local desktop utility.
- The explanatory humility of an excellent teacher.
- The low-friction ritual of a simple focus timer.

### Avoid becoming

- Notion as an endlessly configurable workspace.
- Todoist or TickTick as a generic task manager.
- Motion as an opaque auto-scheduler.
- Forest or Study Bunny as a reward loop.
- ChatGPT as an unbounded conversation surface.
- A social study platform driven by comparison.

## Experience over time

### After the first session

The student should understand the contract: Astra helps them choose, focus, record, and decide what is next. The product should feel personal without pretending to know them. They leave with one correctly recorded learning action and a clear next step.

### After the tenth session

The student should spend less time configuring than on the first day. Astra should reflect simple factual patterns: unfinished actions, planned revision, and recent outcomes. The student should begin to trust that returning is easier than starting from scratch.

### After the hundredth session

The learning record should be genuinely useful. Astra can show which parts of the syllabus have evidence of practice or revision, where progress is partial, and why a recommendation matters. The student should be able to correct any mistaken interpretation.

### After the thousandth session

Astra should feel almost invisible. It holds a durable, exportable record of the student's journey, makes re-entry easy, and provides concise context only when it reduces work. The student should never fear losing the record or being trapped by it.

## Identity test

Before shipping a product surface, ask:

1. Does it make the next learning action clearer, focus calmer, or the learning record more trustworthy?
2. Would a student understand what Astra knows, what it does not know, and why it is suggesting this?
3. Does it protect agency and attention?
4. Could the feature remain useful if all AI were unavailable?

If the answer to any required question is no, the feature does not belong in the current experience.
